## Interpretation (honest reading)

- **MLX-GPU is faster than JAX-CPU in every one of the 12 cells**
  (1.1x–4.8x), and decisively beats MLX-CPU everywhere (2.1x–14x) —
  the GPU is genuinely doing the work. Neither pre-registered fail
  condition triggers. But the >=3x-at-both-N bar is cleared only by
  LGSSM-1D (4.0x / 4.8x).
- **The limiter at large N is full-history materialization, not
  compute.** SV/10⁶ peaks at 12.1 GB (lag-4) and 15.9 GB (async) —
  a third to nearly half of system RAM — because the posterior
  stores (T, N, d) particles + weights + ancestors for T=500. The
  anomalous lag-4 SV cell (2.33 s vs 1.57 s for lag-0/lag-8) and
  TRACK's lag-8/async being ~1.7x faster than lag-4 are
  allocation/memory-pressure artifacts of the same cause. The
  O(1)-memory `store_history`/trace option (already on the
  coordinated-change list) is now the single highest-leverage
  performance item — it would shrink SV/TRACK peaks by ~10x and
  likely move both workloads toward the 3x bar.
- **Cadence**: pure async wins most cells at this workload size
  (histories dominate memory anyway, so its pinned-buffer downside
  is diluted). The lag-k default remains correct for the degeneracy
  contract; revisit after store_history lands.
- Protocol guard honored: the 10⁴ rows show MLX ahead but modestly —
  dispatch overhead territory, as predicted.
